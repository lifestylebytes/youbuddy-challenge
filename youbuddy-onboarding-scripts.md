## SCRIPT 1 · App Walkthrough (앱 사용법, 영어로 하나씩)

**Intro**
Hi everyone, and welcome to YouBuddy! Let me walk you through the app, step by step. Don't worry — it's simple, and I'll show you exactly where to tap.

**1) Your dashboard (대시보드)**
First, this is your dashboard. Think of it as your home base. At the top, you'll see your progress — how many days you've done, and your streak. The streak is your best friend: every day you check in, it grows. So the goal is simple — don't break the streak.

**2) Open today's learning (오늘의 학습 열기)**
Every day, you start here. Tap "Open today's learning." You'll see today's three expressions — real phrases we actually use in foreign-company meetings, emails, and messages. Not textbook English. The English your coworkers really use.

**3) Write your own sentence (표현 3개 작문)**
Now, this is the most important step. Don't just read the expressions. For each one, write your own sentence — a sentence from YOUR real work situation. Type it right into the worksheet. This is how you turn "an expression I know" into "a sentence I can actually say."

**4) Take the word test (단어 테스트)**
Next, take the word test. You get three tries. If you miss one, no problem — it simply comes back so you review it. The test isn't about your score. It's about repeating the words until they stick.

**5) Check in on KakaoTalk (카톡 인증)**
After that, post your check-in in our KakaoTalk group. A quick photo or a short note is enough. This is your daily proof, and it keeps the whole group moving together. When you post, you'll feel it — you're not doing this alone.

**6) Check the Streak Board (데일리 스트릭 보드)**
Come back to the dashboard and open the Daily Streak Board. You'll see everyone's progress, with names hidden. When you see the group showing up every day, it pulls you along too.

**7) Open My PT Studio (내 PT 스튜디오)**
Whenever you want, open "My PT Studio." This is where all your vocabulary and your script live in one place. And here's the best part: everything you build, you keep — forever. Even after the challenge ends.

**8) (Premium) Weekly Zoom meetup (프리미엄 · 주 1회 Zoom)**
If you're in Premium, once a week we meet on Zoom. You'll bring your script, say it out loud, and get real, honest feedback — the kind you can't get at your own company.

**Closing**
So that's it. Every day: open the app, do today's learning, write one sentence, take the test, and check in. Just twenty days. Let's get started — I'll be right here with you.

## SCRIPT 2 · Opening: How to Build & Prepare Your Script (내 대본 쓰는 법, 영어로)

**The one big idea**
Before we start, let me explain the one idea behind this whole challenge. You're not here to "study English." You're here to build your own script — the exact sentences you'll actually say at work.

**Why this works**
Here's the thing: business English is smaller than you think. You don't need every word in the dictionary. You only need the sentences YOU use — in your meetings, your emails, your updates. Once those become automatic, you stop freezing.

**How to write your script (매일)**
So here's how it works, every single day:

1. You get three real expressions.
2. For each one, don't memorize the example — rewrite it for YOUR situation. Change the topic, the project, the name. Make it yours.
   - Example: the expression is *"Let me circle back on that."*
   - Your version: *"Let me circle back on the Q3 budget after I check with finance."*
3. Say it out loud. If it flows, it's ready. If you stumble, fix it until it feels natural in your mouth.
4. Save it. Over four weeks, these sentences pile up into your personal script — sixty-plus lines you can actually use.

**How to prepare & practice**
To get ready, do three simple things:

- Read your saved sentences out loud every morning. Just two minutes.
- Before a real meeting, open your script and pick the lines you'll need that day.
- In our Zoom sessions (Premium), bring your script and practice it live.

The goal is not a perfect accent. The goal is this: when the moment comes, the sentence is already in your mouth.

**Closing**
So remember the four steps: collect the expression, rewrite it as your own, say it out loud, and keep it. That's your script. Let's build it together — starting today.
